# Claims

Claim 1: Visible geometry is an incomplete state representation for assembly manipulation when hidden fixtures constrain motion.

Claim 2: Probe outcome residuals can support an interpretable latent fixture state that improves action selection.

Claim 3: In the v3 full-scale synthetic suite, geometry-only manipulation is unsafe: 24.0% aggregate success and 68.9% damage.

Claim 4: Forced nearest known-taxonomy classification is better than geometry-only but remains brittle: 31.6% success and 60.6% damage.

Claim 5: Guarded abstention improves the safety tradeoff: 44.0% success, 33.3% damage, and 28.1% abstention.

Claim 6: Active probing and POMDP-style belief improve deployable success by using action-relevant information before release.

Claim 7: Oracle fixture knowledge reaches 93.1% success and 0.3% damage, exposing the value of accurate fixture and release-action models.

Claim 8: The result does not establish real-robot performance. The supported claim is a bounded mechanism claim about the usefulness of fixture variables, unknown detection, safe probes, and release-action menus.
