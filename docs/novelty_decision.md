# Novelty Decision

Decision: proceed as a final full-scale synthetic mechanism paper, not as a
hardware benchmark paper.

The strongest version is not larger perception, more data in isolation, or a
new planner. It is a different state variable for manipulation: latent fixture
cause. A robot that sees a part fail to move should ask whether it is bolted,
latched, adhered, blocked by a hidden stop, constrained by a tie, held by a
magnet, multiply constrained, or actually free. That hidden cause changes the
next safe action.

The v3 suite hardens the mechanism beyond the original toy setting. It covers
8 fixture families, 10 regimes, 12 policies, 80 seeds, and 12,288,000
represented fixture-reasoning decisions. The novelty boundary remains honest:
the paper does not claim hardware validation, learned taxonomy discovery, or a
complete POMDP replacement. It claims that fixture variables, unknown
detection, safe probes, and release-action menus form a useful action
interface for hidden-constraint manipulation.
